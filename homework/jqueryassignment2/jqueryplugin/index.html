Norway—Points of Interest



Norway is a geographically diverse and historically amazing country located in Scandinavia. About half of the country lies in the arctic circle with a very rugged and mountainous landscape, with an endless amount of beautiful fjords and picturesque views. The arctic latitude also means that, if the conditions are right, the beautiful Aurora Borealis can also be witnessed.

The southern part of Norway, which is also the most populated, features the capital city of Oslo and other cities such as Bergen and Stavanger.


Below are some points of interest in Norway.

The capital city of Oslo

A fjord located in the heart of northern Norway

Longyearbyen, an arctic settlement on the island of Svalbard, an island located about halfway between mainland Norway and the North Pole

The Kirkenes Snowhotel, a hotel made entirely out of ice located near the Finnish and Russian Border to the northeast

The Aurora Borealis on display at Lofoten

Trondheim, Norway
